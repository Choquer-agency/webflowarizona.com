## How to Choose a Webflow Agency in San Francisco

### Cutting Through the Noise

San Francisco has so many agencies claiming Webflow expertise that evaluation can feel overwhelming. Here's a framework that works:

**Start with certification.** Webflow's Certified Expert Partner program is the fastest way to filter. It doesn't guarantee a good fit, but it eliminates agencies that lack verified platform expertise.

**Check live sites, not portfolios.** Every agency's portfolio page looks impressive. Open the actual sites in a new tab. Run them through PageSpeed Insights. Check them on your phone. Load them on a slow connection. The gap between portfolio screenshots and live site performance tells you a lot.

**Ask about their SF client base.** An agency that has worked with multiple San Francisco companies understands the local market dynamics — the competition for attention, the investor scrutiny, the talent acquisition function of your website. This context matters more than you might think.

### The Five Essential Questions for SF Companies

1. **What's your average Lighthouse performance score?** In San Francisco's competitive search landscape, slow sites die. Accept nothing below 90, and prefer agencies that consistently deliver 95+.

2. **How many Webflow projects have you completed?** Volume of experience matters. A 150+ project portfolio means an agency has encountered and solved most technical challenges you'll face. An agency with 20 projects may still be learning.

3. **What's your typical timeline for a Series A/B company website?** If the answer is "3-6 months," they're not experienced enough with Webflow. Skilled agencies deliver in 3-6 weeks because Webflow's development speed makes longer timelines unnecessary for most projects.

4. **How do you handle ongoing iteration after launch?** PLG companies iterate constantly. Your agency should have a model for post-launch optimization — whether that's a retainer, sprint-based engagements, or thorough training so your team can iterate independently.

5. **Can you show me results, not just designs?** Ask for conversion metrics, SEO improvements, or page speed before/after comparisons from previous projects. Agencies that measure outcomes will happily share data. Those that only talk about aesthetics may not be measuring what matters.

For most startups through Series B, hiring an agency is more capital-efficient than building in-house web design capability. ### How do I evaluate a Webflow agency's technical quality?

Open their portfolio sites and run them through Google PageSpeed Insights. Check Core Web Vitals scores on mobile. Test the sites on a throttled connection. Look for consistent Lighthouse scores above 90 — ideally 95+. Also check whether the sites use clean, semantic HTML (right-click, View Source) and whether accessibility basics like alt text, heading hierarchy, and keyboard navigation are properly implemented. Technical quality is measurable, so don't accept subjective claims when objective data is available.
