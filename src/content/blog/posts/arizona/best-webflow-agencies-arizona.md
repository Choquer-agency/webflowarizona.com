## What Should an Arizona Webflow Project Look Like?

A well-run Webflow project with an Arizona agency should follow this timeline:

**Week 1:** Discovery and strategy session. The agency should ask about your business goals, target audience, competitive landscape, and technical requirements. They should not be asking you to "just send your content" before understanding what you need.

**Weeks 2-3:** Information architecture and wireframes. Before any visual design begins, the site's structure, page hierarchy, and conversion flows should be mapped. This is where SEO strategy gets baked in.

**Weeks 3-4:** Visual design. High-fidelity mockups for key pages, with revisions included in the scope. You should see desktop and mobile versions.

**Weeks 4-6:** Webflow development. The actual build, including CMS setup, responsive behavior, interactions, and integrations.

**Week 6:** Testing and launch. Cross-browser testing, performance optimization, SEO verification, 301 redirect mapping (for redesigns), and a structured launch.

**Post-launch:** At minimum, 30 days of bug fixes. Ideally, training on the Webflow CMS so your team can manage content independently.

Any agency that compresses this into less than 3 weeks for a standard project (5-15 pages) is cutting corners. Any agency that stretches it beyond 10 weeks without a clear reason is padding the timeline.
