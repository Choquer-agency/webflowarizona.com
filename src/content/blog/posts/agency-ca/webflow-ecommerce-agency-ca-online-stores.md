## Canada's Tax Complexity: GST, HST, PST, and QST

Canada's sales tax system is arguably more complex than any single U.S. state's. The federal Goods and Services Tax (GST) of 5% applies nationwide, but provincial taxes vary:

| Province | Tax System | Combined Rate |
|---|---|---|
| Ontario | HST (harmonized) | 13% |
| British Columbia | GST + PST | 12% (5% + 7%) |
| Quebec | GST + QST | 14.975% (5% + 9.975%) |
| Alberta | GST only | 5% |
| Saskatchewan | GST + PST | 11% (5% + 6%) |
| Manitoba | GST + PST | 12% (5% + 7%) |
| Nova Scotia | HST | 15% |
| New Brunswick | HST | 15% |
| PEI | HST | 15% |
| Newfoundland | HST | 15% |

This creates three different tax system types operating simultaneously — HST provinces (one combined tax), GST + PST provinces (two separate taxes), and GST-only provinces. For e-commerce, you need to calculate and display the correct tax based on the buyer's province.

Webflow handles this through TaxJar's Canadian tax module. Configuration requires setting up all provincial rates and specifying which products are taxable (some categories — like basic groceries and children's clothing — are exempt from GST/HST). This initial setup is more involved than U.S. stores, but once configured, it operates automatically.

### Quebec's Language Requirements

Quebec's Consumer Protection Act requires that online stores serving Quebec customers display product information in French (or at minimum, both French and English). This isn't just good practice — it's law. Webflow's CMS can support bilingual content through separate language fields in CMS collections, with a language toggle in the site's interface.

### Canadian Dollar and Pricing

Webflow E-Commerce supports multi-currency pricing, but the implementation requires attention. Canadian stores need to:
- Display prices in CAD with the "$" symbol (potentially with "CAD" designation to avoid confusion with USD)
- Configure payment processing in Canadian dollars through Stripe
- Set shipping rates in CAD
- Handle tax display in CAD

Webflow's Stripe integration supports CAD natively. Product prices are set in your primary currency, and the checkout processes in that currency. For Canadian brands selling exclusively to Canadian customers, this is straightforward. For brands selling to both Canadian and American customers, the single-currency limitation means you'll need to decide on a primary currency or use a workaround.

### Bilingual Commerce for Quebec and Federal Compliance

Canada's Official Languages Act and Quebec's Charter of the French Language create bilingual requirements that affect e-commerce. At minimum, Canadian brands selling nationally should offer French-language product descriptions and checkout experiences.

Webflow handles bilingual content through CMS-driven language switching — each product has English and French description fields, and the interface toggles between them. This produces a more polished bilingual experience than Shopify's Langify app, which often feels bolted on.

## Setting Up Webflow E-Commerce for Canada

### Payment Processing in CAD

Configure Stripe for Canadian dollar processing. Stripe supports CAD natively and handles Interac Online (Canada's domestic debit system) for Canadian buyers. Ensure product prices display with "CAD" designation or a Canadian flag indicator to avoid USD confusion.

### Shipping With Canada Post

Webflow doesn't have native Canada Post integration (unlike Shopify). For Canadian-focused stores, your options:
- **Flat-rate shipping tiers** by province or region (simplest approach)
- **Third-party shipping calculator** that pulls Canada Post rates (Easyship, ShipStation)
- **Free shipping threshold** that eliminates rate calculation entirely for orders above a minimum

For most Canadian DTC brands with lightweight products (fashion, beauty, food), flat-rate tiers work well and avoid the complexity of real-time rate calculation.

### Cross-Border Selling (Canada to U.S.)

Many Canadian brands sell to U.S. customers. Webflow handles this through:
- Single-currency pricing (CAD) with Stripe converting at checkout for U.S. cards
- TaxJar calculating U.S. state taxes for American deliveries
- Shipping rate configuration for international (Canada-to-U.S.) delivery

The UX isn't as polished as Shopify Markets (which auto-detects location and shows local pricing), but it's functional for brands where U.S. sales supplement Canadian revenue.

### Bilingual Implementation

For brands serving Quebec (legally required) or choosing to offer French nationally:
1. Create bilingual CMS fields for all product content (title_en, title_fr, description_en, description_fr)
2. Build a language toggle in the site navigation
3. Use Webflow's conditional visibility to show the appropriate language content
4. Ensure checkout language follows the user's selection

This is more manual than Shopify's translation apps but produces a higher-quality bilingual experience.

### How does Webflow handle Canada's GST/HST/PST system?

Through TaxJar integration. Configure TaxJar with all provincial rates — 13% HST for Ontario, 12% for BC (5% GST + 7% PST), 14.975% for Quebec (5% GST + 9.975% QST), and so on. TaxJar determines the buyer's province from the shipping address and applies the correct rate. The initial setup is more involved than Shopify's built-in Canadian tax, but once configured, it operates automatically.

### Can I build a bilingual French/English store on Webflow?

Yes — and Webflow produces higher-quality bilingual experiences than Shopify's translation apps. Use CMS collections with dual-language fields (English and French) and a language toggle in the navigation. The approach requires more initial setup but creates a native-feeling bilingual experience rather than the machine-translated feel that Shopify's Langify or Weglot apps sometimes produce. For Quebec compliance, this quality matters.

### What payment methods do Canadian customers expect?

Canadian consumers expect credit/debit cards (Visa, Mastercard), Interac (Canada's domestic debit network), and increasingly Apple Pay and Google Pay. Webflow's Stripe integration supports Visa, Mastercard, American Express, Apple Pay, and Google Pay. Interac Online support depends on Stripe's current capabilities in Canada — verify this before launching, as Interac is widely used for online purchases.

### Is Webflow E-Commerce cost-effective in Canadian dollars?

Webflow's plans are priced in USD, which means Canadian merchants pay approximately $58–$325 CAD/month (at typical exchange rates). Shopify's plans are also USD-based but with Canadian pricing tiers. When factoring in Webflow's included design customization (which would cost extra on Shopify through themes, apps, or Plus plan), Webflow often costs less for Canadian brands that prioritize design quality. The 0% transaction fee on Webflow's Business plan provides additional savings compared to Shopify's 0.5–2% fee.
