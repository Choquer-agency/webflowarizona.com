## How to Choose the Right Webflow Agency for Your Canadian Firm

### What Certifications and Track Record Should You Look For?

The most important credential is Webflow's official Expert Partner or Enterprise Partner certification. This is not a badge anyone can buy — it requires a demonstrated portfolio of high-quality Webflow projects and passing Webflow's review process.

Beyond certification, ask for:

- **Lighthouse performance scores** from live client sites (not demo projects)
- **Specific professional services experience.** Building a law firm website is fundamentally different from building an e-commerce store. Practice area architectures, attorney bio systems, and thought leadership platforms require specialized knowledge.
- **Canadian compliance experience.** PIPEDA, AODA, Quebec's Law 25, and CASL (Canada's Anti-Spam Legislation) all affect how professional services websites collect data, display content, and communicate with prospects.

### How Important Is Bilingual Capability?

If your firm operates nationally or serves Quebec clients, bilingual capability is not optional — it is a business requirement. Under Quebec's Charter of the French Language (Bill 96, updated 2022), businesses serving Quebec consumers must make their websites available in French.

Look for agencies that build true bilingual architectures with:

- Proper hreflang tags for SEO
- Independent CMS collections for each language (not machine translation)
- Language switching that preserves page context
- Separate metadata and schema markup for each language version

### What Should a Professional Services Webflow Site Include?

At minimum, your firm's Webflow site should include:

1. **Practice area pages** with detailed service descriptions, relevant team members, and case studies
2. **Attorney/team directory** with individual bio pages, filterable by practice area, office, and seniority
3. **Thought leadership platform** — a blog or insights section with proper categorization and author attribution
4. **Contact and intake flows** with appropriate disclaimers and privacy notices
5. **Schema markup** for LocalBusiness, Attorney (if applicable), and Article types

### What Does PIPEDA Compliance Mean for Your Website?

Canada's Personal Information Protection and Electronic Documents Act (PIPEDA) requires organizations to obtain meaningful consent before collecting personal information. For your website, this means:

- Clear privacy policies explaining what data you collect and why
- Consent mechanisms for contact forms, newsletter signups, and cookie tracking
- Data retention policies and the ability to respond to access requests
- Special considerations if you transfer data outside Canada (relevant if using US-based analytics or CRM tools)

Quebec's Law 25 (fully effective September 2024) adds additional requirements including privacy impact assessments and a designated privacy officer. Your web agency should understand these requirements and build compliance into the site architecture, not bolt it on after launch.
