**Choosing a web design agency in Texas requires navigating one of the largest agency markets in the country — narrow your search by metro focus (Austin, Dallas, Houston, or San Antonio), verify industry-specific experience in energy, tech, healthcare, or your sector, and insist on seeing measurable results from past Texas clients before signing.** With thousands of agencies operating across the state, the hardest part isn't finding options — it's filtering them.

Texas has the second-largest economy of any U.S. state, and the Texas Workforce Commission reports that the state's professional and business services sector — which includes web design, marketing, and tech services — employed over 1.8 million workers as of late 2025. That means more agencies competing for your business than almost anywhere else in the country. The paradox of choice is real, and it leads to two common outcomes: decision paralysis (you keep researching and never hire anyone) or hasty decisions (you pick the first agency that gives a confident pitch).

This guide gives you a systematic approach to cut through the noise. Whether you're a Houston energy company, an Austin SaaS startup, a Dallas professional services firm, or a San Antonio healthcare practice, the framework below will help you find the right partner — not just the loudest one.

## Why Texas Makes Agency Selection Uniquely Complex

### Four Major Metros, Four Different Markets

Texas isn't one market. It's at least four, each with its own economic drivers and agency ecosystem:

**Austin**: Tech-heavy, startup-oriented, design-forward. Agencies here tend to skew toward SaaS, startups, and creative brands. Expect higher prices and trendier design sensibilities. The downside? Some Austin agencies prioritize aesthetics over conversion fundamentals.

**Dallas-Fort Worth**: Corporate, finance, real estate, professional services. DFW agencies often excel at polished corporate sites and enterprise-level projects. The agency scene is mature, with established shops that have been operating for 15+ years.

**Houston**: Energy, healthcare, manufacturing, logistics, international trade. Houston agencies frequently have experience with complex industries and regulatory-sensitive content. The oil and gas sector's digital transformation has created agencies with deep technical expertise.

**San Antonio**: Military, healthcare, tourism, growing tech scene. Often overlooked, SA has a strong mid-market agency community that offers competitive pricing without sacrificing quality. The presence of military installations and healthcare systems creates agencies comfortable with compliance-heavy work.

### Industry Diversity Demands Specialization

Texas's economy isn't dominated by a single sector. You'll find agencies that specialize in:

- Oil and gas / energy sector websites
- Healthcare and medical practice marketing
- Real estate and property development
- SaaS and technology companies
- Agriculture and ranching operations
- Legal and professional services
- Hospitality and tourism
- Construction and trades

The agency that's perfect for a Midland oil services company is probably wrong for a Fredericksburg winery. Industry alignment matters more in Texas than in most states because the industries are so different in their requirements, audiences, and regulatory contexts.

### Scale Expectations Vary Wildly

A "small business" in Texas might be a 3-person consulting firm in Boerne or a 200-employee manufacturing company in Irving. Agencies calibrate their services differently, and a mismatch in scale expectations leads to the most common complaints:

- Small businesses hiring agencies built for enterprise (overpriced, overengineered)
- Mid-market companies hiring freelancers or small shops (under-resourced, unreliable)

Be honest about your company's size, budget, and complexity when you start conversations.

## The Texas Agency Evaluation Playbook

### Step 1: Define Your Metro and Industry Filter

Before you Google "web design agency Texas," narrow your parameters:

1. **Which metro's economy does your business operate in?** This determines which agency pool to draw from.
2. **What industry are you in?** This determines what portfolio experience to require.
3. **What's your realistic budget?** This determines which tier of agency to approach.

These three filters will reduce thousands of options to a manageable shortlist of 5-10 agencies.

### Step 2: Assess Portfolio With Skepticism

Every agency shows their best work. That's fine. But dig deeper:

- **Ask about projects that aren't in the portfolio.** Agencies typically show 10-15% of their work. The unlisted projects tell you more about their average output.
- **Check if portfolio sites are still live.** If a site they built two years ago is now on a completely different platform, ask why the client switched.
- **Look at mobile versions.** Many agency portfolios show desktop screenshots only. Pull up the actual sites on your phone.
- **Verify industry relevance.** A beautiful portfolio means nothing if it's all fashion brands and you sell industrial equipment.

### Step 3: Evaluate Their Technical Foundation

The platform question is especially important in Texas because the state's industry diversity creates diverse technical needs:

**For service businesses and SMBs:**
Webflow is increasingly the right choice — fast, visually editable, no ongoing developer dependency for content updates. [Learn about our Webflow approach](/).

**For enterprise and complex applications:**
Custom development or headless CMS architectures may be justified, but only if the complexity is real.

**For e-commerce:**
Shopify for straightforward product sales, Webflow for design-first product brands, custom for complex B2B commerce.

**For content-heavy organizations:**
WordPress remains viable if properly maintained, but the maintenance burden is real — ask who handles security patches and plugin updates.

Press any agency on *why* they recommend their platform. "It's what we know" is honest but not necessarily in your interest.

### Step 4: Demand Business Metrics, Not Just Design Awards

Texas is a results-oriented state. Your agency should speak in business terms:

- "We increased their lead form submissions by 340% in the first quarter."
- "Page load time dropped from 6.2 seconds to 1.8, and bounce rate decreased 28%."
- "Their Google Business Profile visibility increased 65% after launch."

If all they can tell you is "the client loved it," that's a design studio, not a business partner. Both have their place — but know which one you're hiring.

### Step 5: Understand Their Process and Communication

The best Texas agencies follow a structured process. Here's what to look for:

**Discovery phase**: Do they spend time understanding your business, or do they jump to wireframes? The former is a strategic agency; the latter is an order-taker.

**Communication cadence**: Weekly check-ins are the minimum standard. Ask about their project management approach — do they use tools you can access, or do updates come as occasional emails?

**Revision process**: How many rounds of revisions are included? What happens when scope changes? Get this in writing.

**Timeline commitment**: For most Texas SMB sites, expect 8-12 weeks. Enterprise sites may take 16-24 weeks. Be cautious of both extremes.

### Step 6: Clarify Pricing and Ownership

Texas agency pricing varies enormously by metro and agency tier:

| Agency Tier | Typical Range | What You Get |
|---|---|---|
| Freelancer/micro | $2,000 - $5,000 | Basic site, limited strategy, variable reliability |
| Small agency (2-10 people) | $5,000 - $15,000 | Custom design, some strategy, dedicated attention |
| Mid-size agency | $15,000 - $50,000 | Full strategy, custom design/dev, content, SEO |
| Large/enterprise agency | $50,000 - $200,000+ | Multi-stakeholder process, enterprise integrations, ongoing retainer |

Austin agencies tend to charge 15-25% more than equivalent agencies in San Antonio or Houston for comparable work. Factor location-based pricing into your comparisons.

**Ownership essentials:**
- You own the domain name (registered in your name/company)
- You own or have full access to hosting credentials
- You receive all source files upon project completion
- Content (copy, images, video) belongs to you
- No proprietary lock-in that prevents you from switching agencies

### Step 7: Check References With Purpose

Don't just ask "were you happy?" Ask reference clients:

- "What was the most challenging part of the project?"
- "Did the final cost match the initial quote? If not, why?"
- "How quickly did they respond when something broke after launch?"
- "Has the website measurably improved your business results?"
- "Knowing what you know now, would you choose the same agency?"

Two strong references beat ten glowing Google reviews. Reviews can be gamed; conversations with real clients are much harder to fake.

## Texas-Specific Red Flags

### The "We Do Everything" Agency

Texas is big enough to support specialized agencies. If a shop claims to be expert in web design, SEO, PPC, social media, PR, video production, app development, and branding — they're either enormous or they're overpromising. Specialization is a strength, not a limitation.

### The Low-Ball Energy Sector Play

Some agencies specifically target Houston energy companies with artificially low initial quotes, planning to make up the difference in change orders and ongoing retainers. If an oil and gas website quote seems too good to be true, it is.

### No Bilingual Capability

Depending on your market, Spanish-language capability may be essential. The Texas demographic reality means many businesses — especially in San Antonio, El Paso, the Rio Grande Valley, and large portions of Houston — serve significant Spanish-speaking populations. If your agency can't address this need or dismisses it, they don't understand your market.

### Portfolio Is All From One Client

Some small agencies have one big client that represents 70% of their revenue. That's fine for them, but risky for you — if that client demands attention during your project, you'll get deprioritized.

## The Decision Framework

After evaluating your shortlist, score each agency on these ten factors (1-5 scale):

1. **Industry relevance** — Do they know your sector?
2. **Portfolio quality** — Are their past projects genuinely good and functional?
3. **Technical competence** — Do they recommend the right platform with clear reasoning?
4. **Business orientation** — Do they talk about results, not just design?
5. **Communication quality** — Were they responsive and clear during the sales process?
6. **Pricing transparency** — Is the scope of work detailed and comprehensive?
7. **Reference strength** — Did their clients validate their claims?
8. **Process maturity** — Do they have a clear, structured approach?
9. **Ownership clarity** — Will you own everything when it's done?
10. **Cultural fit** — Do you trust and enjoy working with these people?

## FAQ

### How do I choose between agencies in different Texas metros?

Geography matters less than industry expertise. A Houston agency with deep energy sector experience is the right choice for an oil and gas company, even if you're based in Dallas. That said, if your project involves on-site photography, in-person strategy sessions, or location-specific content, proximity has practical value. Remote collaboration works well when communication systems are strong.

### What's the average web design cost in Texas?

For small to mid-size businesses, $5,000-$15,000 is the most common range for a professionally designed, strategically sound website. Austin tends toward the higher end of the range, while San Antonio and mid-size Texas cities often offer competitive pricing. Enterprise projects with complex integrations can range from $25,000 to well over $100,000.

### Should a Texas business prioritize SEO when choosing an agency?

Absolutely. Given the competitive density in major Texas metros — where dozens or hundreds of similar businesses compete for the same local search queries — your agency must have a concrete local SEO strategy. This includes Google Business Profile optimization, city-specific landing pages, schema markup, and content strategy. An agency that treats SEO as an afterthought or an add-on service will leave significant revenue on the table.

### How important is bilingual website capability in Texas?

Depending on your market, it may be essential. Over 29% of Texans speak Spanish at home, and in markets like San Antonio, El Paso, and South Texas, that percentage is significantly higher. At minimum, discuss with your agency whether a Spanish-language version or bilingual content would better serve your customers. Not every business needs it, but dismissing it without consideration is a missed opportunity.

### What's the biggest difference between Austin and Houston agencies?

Austin agencies generally lean toward creative, design-forward, startup-oriented work. Houston agencies tend toward industrial, corporate, and complex-system projects. Neither is inherently better — the right fit depends on your business type. A tech startup will likely resonate with Austin's agency culture; a manufacturing company may find Houston agencies more aligned with their needs and communication style.

## Final Advice

Texas gives you more options than almost any other state. That's a luxury, but only if you use it wisely. Don't default to the agency your business friend recommended without doing your own evaluation. Don't choose based on geography alone. And don't let a slick sales presentation substitute for verified results.

The framework in this guide works whether you're a 5-person business in Lubbock or a 500-person company in downtown Dallas. Start with your filters, evaluate systematically, and trust the data over the pitch.
