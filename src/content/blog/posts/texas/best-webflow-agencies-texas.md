## How to Choose the Right Webflow Agency in Texas

Not every agency on this list is right for every project. Here is a framework for narrowing down your choice based on what actually matters.

### Define Your Primary Goal

Before you contact a single agency, get clear on what success looks like:

- **Lead generation?** You need an agency that thinks about conversion architecture, not just visual design. Look for agencies that talk about form strategy, page speed, and SEO — not just brand aesthetics.
- **Brand launch or rebrand?** Design-forward agencies like Barrel or Covet Studio will prioritize the visual identity.
- **Platform migration?** You need an agency that has done dozens of migrations and can show you the process. Our [migration services](/services/wordpress-to-webflow-migration/) are purpose-built for this.
- **Enterprise or complex integration?** Technical depth matters more than design awards.

### Evaluate Their Webflow Expertise Specifically

Many agencies list Webflow as one of a dozen platforms they support. That is a red flag. The best Webflow agencies build exclusively or primarily on Webflow because it takes deep platform knowledge to leverage its full capability. Ask these questions:

- Are you a Certified Webflow Expert Partner?
- What percentage of your projects are built on Webflow?
- Can you show me Lighthouse scores from sites you have delivered?
- How do you handle Webflow CMS architecture for content-heavy sites?

A portfolio of beautiful screenshots tells you nothing about site performance. Before hiring any agency, run their portfolio sites through Google PageSpeed Insights. If their showcase work does not score above 85, imagine what their less prominent projects look like.

## What Should You Expect From the Process?

A professional Webflow agency in Texas should follow a process that looks roughly like this:

1. **Discovery call (free):** 30-minute conversation about your goals, timeline, and budget. Any agency that will not get on a call before quoting is not worth your time.
2. **Proposal and scope:** A clear document outlining deliverables, timeline, pricing, and what is included. Watch out for vague "phase 2" language that hides additional costs.
3. **Design phase:** Wireframes first, then high-fidelity mockups. You should have at least two rounds of revisions included.
4. **Development:** The Webflow build itself, including CMS setup, responsive design, interactions, and integrations.
5. **Testing and launch:** Cross-browser testing, performance optimization, SEO verification, and a structured launch process.
6. **Post-launch support:** At minimum, 30 days of bug fixes. Ideally, an ongoing relationship for optimization.

If an agency skips the discovery phase or jumps straight to "send us your content and we'll build it," that is a warning sign.

Either can work, but local Texas agencies offer advantages for businesses that value in-person collaboration and local market knowledge.
