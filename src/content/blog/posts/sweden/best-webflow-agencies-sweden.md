## Why Swedish Companies Are Choosing Webflow Over WordPress

### The Swedish Design Philosophy Aligns Perfectly with Webflow

Swedish design — whether you trace it to the Bauhaus-influenced functionalism of the 1930s or the modern legacy of IKEA — prioritizes clarity, functionality, and restraint. Webflow's visual development environment maps naturally onto these values. Unlike WordPress, where themes impose structure and plugins add bloat, Webflow gives designers direct control over every element without writing code.

This matters in Sweden specifically because Swedish users have extremely high expectations for digital experiences. Sweden ranks consistently in the top five globally for internet penetration and digital literacy. According to Internetstiftelsen's annual Svenskarna och internet report, over 95% of Swedes aged 16-65 use the internet daily. They notice when a site feels sluggish or when the design lacks intentionality.

### Accessibility is Non-Negotiable in Sweden

Sweden's accessibility culture runs deep. The Diskrimineringslagen (Discrimination Act) and the EU's European Accessibility Act both apply to Swedish businesses, and enforcement is getting stricter. Webflow's semantic HTML output and built-in accessibility features make compliance significantly easier than retrofitting a WordPress site with accessibility plugins.

For companies serving the Swedish public sector — or any business wanting to sell to kommuner and landsting — meeting WCAG 2.1 AA is a procurement requirement, not a nice-to-have. The best Webflow agencies in Sweden build accessibility into every project from wireframe to launch.

## How to Choose the Right Webflow Agency in Sweden

### What Webflow Certification Actually Means

Webflow's certification program isn't just a badge. Certified Expert Partners have been vetted by Webflow for technical proficiency, project quality, and client satisfaction. In Sweden's market, where many agencies claim Webflow expertise after building a few sites, certification is a meaningful differentiator.

When evaluating agencies, ask specifically about their Webflow certification status and how many Webflow projects they've completed. A generalist agency that has built 10 Webflow sites will approach your project very differently from a specialist with 150+.

### Key Questions to Ask Before Signing a Contract

1. **How many Webflow projects have you completed?** Experience matters. Complex CMS structures, custom animations, and third-party integrations all require platform-specific knowledge that only comes from doing the work repeatedly.

2. **What's your average Lighthouse performance score?** This isn't a vanity metric. In Sweden's high-competition digital market, a slow site directly impacts your search rankings and conversion rates. Expect top agencies to consistently deliver scores above 90.

3. **Do you handle accessibility compliance?** Given Sweden's regulatory environment and cultural expectations around accessibility, this should be non-negotiable. Ask for specific examples of WCAG 2.1 AA compliance work.

4. **What's your approach to Swedish-language SEO?** Bilingual sites (Swedish/English) have specific technical requirements around hreflang tags, URL structures, and content strategy. An agency experienced in the Swedish market will have a clear process for this.

5. **Can you show me live sites, not just screenshots?** Portfolio screenshots can be deceiving. Visit the actual sites, run them through PageSpeed Insights, check the mobile experience, and test the CMS editor functionality.

### Red Flags to Watch For

- **No Webflow-specific portfolio.** If an agency shows you WordPress sites and promises "we can do the same in Webflow," proceed with caution.
- **Template-based approaches.** Quality Webflow agencies build custom — they don't start from templates and modify.
- **No performance guarantees.** If an agency can't commit to a minimum Lighthouse score, they're not confident in their technical execution.
- **Unusually long timelines.** A typical Webflow project should take 3-8 weeks depending on complexity. If someone quotes 4-6 months, they're either not experienced with Webflow or padding the timeline.
