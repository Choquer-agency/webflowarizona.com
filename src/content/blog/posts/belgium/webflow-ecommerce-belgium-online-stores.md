## Belgium's E-Commerce Market: Small Country, Complex Requirements

Belgium punches well above its weight in e-commerce. With over 11.5 million people, approximately 70% of Belgians shop online, generating an estimated EUR 15 billion annually. But Belgium's e-commerce landscape is uniquely complex for a country its size.

The complexity starts with language. Belgium has three official languages — Dutch (Flanders), French (Wallonia), and German (Eupen-Malmedy) — and any nationally-focused Belgian business needs at minimum Dutch and French versions of their storefront. This isn't optional; it's a legal and commercial requirement that affects everything from product descriptions to checkout flows.

Then there's Belgium's position as the de facto capital of the European Union. Brussels hosts EU institutions, NATO headquarters, and hundreds of international organizations, creating a uniquely international consumer base that expects multilingual, multi-cultural shopping experiences.

Belgian consumers also have distinct payment preferences. Bancontact (formerly Bancontact/Mister Cash) is the dominant online payment method — used for over 60% of Belgian e-commerce transactions. Not offering Bancontact is like not accepting credit cards in most other countries.

### EUR Processing and Bancontact

Webflow processes EUR through Stripe, which has comprehensive Belgian support. Critically, Stripe supports Bancontact as a payment method in Belgium. This means Webflow stores CAN accept Bancontact payments through Stripe's checkout — a significant advantage that removes what would otherwise be a dealbreaker for Belgian e-commerce.

This is worth emphasizing: unlike Vipps in Norway or TWINT in Switzerland, Bancontact is available through Stripe, which means Webflow has no payment method gap for the Belgian market.

### Belgian BTW/TVA Configuration

Belgium's VAT (BTW in Dutch, TVA in French) operates at:
- **Standard rate:** 21%
- **Reduced rate:** 12% (certain food, social housing)
- **Super-reduced rate:** 6% (basic necessities, newspapers, books, water)

Webflow handles this through Stripe Tax or manual rules. Most Belgian online stores selling standard goods apply the 21% rate. Food and book sellers need Stripe Tax for automatic rate differentiation.

### Bancontact Support: Webflow's Belgian Advantage

Unlike some Nordic payment methods that aren't available through Stripe, Bancontact IS fully supported by Stripe. This means Belgian customers shopping on a Webflow-powered store can pay with Bancontact at checkout — the same experience they'd have on a Shopify store with Shopify Payments.

This removes the single largest potential barrier to Webflow adoption in the Belgian market.

### bpost Shipping Integration

bpost (Belgium's national postal service) dominates Belgian e-commerce delivery, including the popular bpost pickup points and Cube lockers. Shopify integrates bpost through dedicated apps. Webflow requires third-party shipping solutions to connect with bpost's API.

For Belgian businesses where bpost pickup point selection is a critical checkout feature, Shopify's more established integration saves development time. However, third-party shipping platforms like Sendcloud (a Netherlands-based solution popular in Belgium) offer Webflow-compatible bpost integration.

## Belgian Legal Compliance

### Code de Droit Économique / Wetboek van Economisch Recht

Belgium's Economic Law Code governs e-commerce with requirements including:
- **14-day withdrawal right** (herroepingsrecht / droit de rétractation) — clearly communicated
- **Pre-contractual information** — identity, price, delivery details before purchase
- **Electronic confirmation** — order confirmation within reasonable time
- **Clear pricing** — all prices must include BTW/TVA

Webflow stores can build all required disclosure pages. The key is content completeness, not platform capability.

### GDPR via Belgian DPA (APD/GBA)

Belgium's Autorité de protection des données / Gegevensbeschermingsautoriteit enforces GDPR with particular attention to cookie consent. Belgium's DPA has been among the most active in Europe on cookie enforcement. Your Webflow store needs:
- Compliant cookie banner (no pre-checked boxes, real choice)
- Comprehensive privacy policy in both NL and FR
- Data processing register
- EU data hosting preference

Webflow supports all technical requirements; compliance is a content and configuration responsibility.

### Belgian Packaging Regulations

Belgium's FOST Plus system requires businesses selling packaged products to participate in packaging waste management. This is a business obligation, not a platform feature, but your e-commerce site should display the Green Dot (Groene Punt) where applicable.

## Frequently Asked Questions

### Can Belgian customers pay with Bancontact on a Webflow store?

Yes. Stripe fully supports Bancontact as a payment method, and since Webflow uses Stripe for checkout, Belgian customers can pay with Bancontact. This is a significant advantage — Bancontact accounts for over 60% of Belgian online payments, making its availability essential for any Belgian e-commerce site.

### Does Webflow support bilingual Belgian stores (Dutch/French)?

Yes. Webflow's CMS localization allows you to create Dutch and French versions of all content — product pages, collection pages, marketing content, and legal pages. You can set up language-specific URLs (/nl/ and /fr/) for SEO purposes. The technical infrastructure is solid; the main investment is creating quality content in both languages.

### How does Webflow handle Belgian BTW/TVA?

Webflow supports Belgian VAT through Stripe Tax or manual tax rules. The standard 21% rate is straightforward to configure. For businesses selling food (12% or 6%), books (6%), or other reduced-rate products, Stripe Tax handles automatic rate differentiation. All prices displayed to Belgian consumers must include BTW/TVA.

### Can I integrate bpost shipping with Webflow?

Not directly, but through third-party platforms like Sendcloud, which is widely used in Belgium and integrates with Webflow. Sendcloud supports bpost, bpost pickup points, and other Belgian carriers. Shopify has more direct bpost app integration, but the Sendcloud route for Webflow is functional and well-established in the Belgian market.

### Is Webflow compliant with Belgian e-commerce regulations?

Webflow provides the technical capabilities for Belgian compliance — custom legal pages, cookie consent, multilingual content. However, Belgian e-commerce law has specific requirements (14-day withdrawal, pre-contractual information, bilingual obligations) that require careful content implementation. These are content responsibilities, not platform limitations. Consider consulting with a Belgian legal advisor to ensure full compliance.
