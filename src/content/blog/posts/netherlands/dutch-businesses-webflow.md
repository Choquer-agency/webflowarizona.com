## Frequently Asked Questions

**Can Webflow support Dutch e-commerce businesses?**
Webflow offers native e-commerce functionality for small to mid-size product catalogues, including product pages, cart, checkout, and Stripe payment processing. For larger Dutch e-commerce operations, Webflow works effectively as the marketing and brand layer while a dedicated e-commerce platform like Shopify handles transactions and inventory. Many Dutch DTC brands use this hybrid approach — Webflow for the brand experience and content marketing, with e-commerce functionality handled by a platform optimized for high-volume transactions. Dutch payment methods like iDEAL can be integrated through Stripe's payment options.

**Is Webflow suitable for large Dutch enterprises?**
Yes. Webflow's Enterprise plan serves large Dutch organizations with features including custom security configurations, SLA guarantees, role-based publishing permissions, and dedicated support. Companies like Rakuten, Lattice, and Upwork use Webflow at scale. For Dutch enterprises, the key advantage is allowing marketing teams to operate independently from IT departments — reducing the internal bureaucracy that slows down website updates in large organizations. Webflow also offers a robust API that enables integration with enterprise systems and custom workflows.

**How does Webflow handle Dutch language SEO?**
Webflow provides full control over the SEO elements needed for Dutch language search optimization — custom title tags, meta descriptions, URL slugs, alt text, and heading hierarchy, all configurable in Dutch. The platform supports hreflang tags for signalling language and regional targeting to search engines, which is critical for Dutch businesses that want to rank in both Dutch (.nl) and Belgian Dutch (.be) search results. Webflow's automatic XML sitemap and clean HTML structure give Dutch businesses a strong technical SEO foundation that most WordPress installations cannot match without extensive plugin configuration.
