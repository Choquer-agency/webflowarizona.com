## Florida's E-Commerce Market: Sun, Tourism, and Rapid Growth

Florida is the third-largest state economy and one of America's fastest-growing e-commerce markets. The state added more new e-commerce business registrations than any state except California and Texas in 2025, driven by population influx, favorable tax policy, and a consumer culture that increasingly shops online to avoid the heat.

Florida's e-commerce landscape breaks into distinct regional economies:

**Miami and South Florida** operate as a gateway to Latin America, producing luxury brands, fashion labels, and lifestyle companies that sell to both domestic and international audiences. Wynwood and the Design District have become incubators for DTC brands that blend Latin American design influences with American commerce infrastructure.

**Orlando and Central Florida** are dominated by tourism — theme park merchandise, attraction-adjacent products, and vacation lifestyle brands generate massive online revenue. But the region also hosts a growing tech sector producing non-tourism DTC brands.

**Tampa Bay and the Gulf Coast** have emerged as a DTC startup corridor. Lower cost of living than Miami and a growing creative class produce brands in wellness, marine accessories, outdoor lifestyle, and specialty food.

**Jacksonville and North Florida** contribute logistics-focused e-commerce operations — the region's port infrastructure and warehouse availability make it a fulfillment hub.

## Florida Sales Tax Configuration

Florida's sales tax is relatively straightforward:
- **State rate**: 6%
- **County discretionary surtax**: 0–2.5% (varies by county)
- **Miami-Dade County combined**: 7%
- **Orange County (Orlando) combined**: 6.5%
- **Hillsborough County (Tampa) combined**: 8.5%

Florida is a destination-based state, so you charge the rate where the buyer receives the product. Webflow's TaxJar integration handles this automatically. The relatively low number of rate variations (67 counties, no city-level taxes) makes Florida's tax setup simpler than states like Colorado or Louisiana.

Notable Florida tax exemptions that affect e-commerce:
- **Clothing under $60**: Exempt during Florida's Back-to-School tax holiday (typically late July)
- **Digital goods**: Generally not taxable in Florida (an advantage for brands selling digital products)
- **Food for home consumption**: Exempt (relevant for specialty food brands shipping edible products)

## Setting Up Webflow E-Commerce for Florida

### Shipping From Florida

Florida's peninsula geography affects shipping economics. You're 1,000+ miles from the Midwest and 1,200+ miles from the West Coast by ground. Most Florida e-commerce brands ship from Jacksonville or Central Florida for better national coverage, or from Miami for Latin American and Caribbean markets.

Webflow shipping configuration for Florida stores:
- **Free in-state shipping** for Florida addresses (competitive advantage since many Florida brands compete with Amazon Prime)
- **Zone-based rates** — Southeast is cheapest, West Coast is most expensive
- **International** if you ship to Caribbean or Latin America (manual configuration in Webflow)
- **Hurricane season notices** — use Webflow's conditional visibility to display shipping delay warnings during June–November

### Hurricane Season E-Commerce Continuity

Florida e-commerce faces a unique risk: hurricane season can disrupt fulfillment for days or weeks. Webflow's static hosting is actually an advantage here — your store stays online even if your local internet goes down, since the site is served from CDN nodes nationwide. Your fulfillment logistics are the vulnerability, not your storefront.

Build hurricane preparation into your Webflow store:
- Conditional banners for shipping delay notifications
- Pre-storm sale events (common for Florida brands clearing seasonal inventory)
- Post-storm recovery messaging

### Bilingual Commerce for South Florida

South Florida's market is substantially bilingual. Building a Webflow store with Spanish language support requires CMS-driven content switching — not just running the site through Google Translate. Webflow's CMS collections can store bilingual product descriptions, and conditional visibility can toggle between languages. It's more work than Shopify's Langify app, but the result is more polished.

For Miami brands where half or more of customers prefer Spanish, this investment in proper bilingual UX pays dividends in conversion rates.

### How does Florida's sales tax work with Webflow E-Commerce?

Florida charges a 6% state rate plus county discretionary surtaxes (0–2.5%). Webflow's TaxJar integration automatically calculates the combined rate based on the buyer's shipping county. Miami-Dade is 7%, Orange County (Orlando) is 6.5%, and Hillsborough (Tampa) is 8.5%. Digital products are generally not taxable in Florida — a notable advantage for brands selling downloads or digital access.
