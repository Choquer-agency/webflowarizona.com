- Bilingual setup (English and Traditional Chinese) with proper URL structure
- Contact and enquiry forms
- Google Analytics 4 and conversion tracking

For a professional services firm in Admiralty, a trading company in Tsim Sha Tsui, or a startup in the HKSTP Science Park, this tier delivers a website that looks and performs at the level of sites built by agencies charging HK$120,000 or more.

The bilingual component deserves specific mention. Nearly every Hong Kong business website must function in both English and Traditional Chinese. ## How Does the Greater Bay Area Affect Website Investment Decisions?

Hong Kong's integration into the Greater Bay Area (GBA) is reshaping how businesses think about their digital presence. A financial advisory firm in Sheung Wan is no longer just serving Hong Kong clients — it is positioning for Shenzhen, Guangzhou, Zhuhai, and the nine other GBA cities with a combined GDP exceeding HK$13 trillion.

This cross-border reality affects website costs in several ways:

### Multilingual Expansion Beyond Bilingual

GBA-focused websites may need Traditional Chinese (for Hong Kong and Macau), Simplified Chinese (for mainland audiences), and English. On WordPress, adding a third language to WPML increases complexity and cost significantly. On Webflow, adding another locale is structurally the same as adding the first — the incremental cost is primarily content translation, not platform configuration.

### China-Accessible Architecture

Websites targeting mainland Chinese audiences face the challenge of the Great Firewall. While Webflow's CDN performs well throughout Hong Kong and the SAR, guaranteed mainland performance requires supplementary infrastructure — typically a mirror site hosted within mainland China on Alibaba Cloud or Tencent Cloud. This adds HK$15,000-HK$40,000 to the project scope but is essential for companies whose GBA strategy depends on mainland web visibility.

### Cross-Border Payment Integration

E-commerce and financial services sites serving GBA clients increasingly need to support multiple payment methods — FPS for Hong Kong, WeChat Pay and Alipay for mainland visitors, and traditional credit card processing for international clients. Webflow's integration capabilities through custom code and Zapier handle these multi-gateway setups, though the development work adds to the project cost.

## What Factors Influence the Final Price?

### Cost Drivers Specific to Hong Kong

- **Bilingual content creation**: If you need copywriting in both English and Traditional Chinese (not just translation), budget an additional 40-60% above a monolingual project
- **Financial services compliance**: SFC-regulated firms, licensed insurance brokerages, and HKMA-supervised entities need compliance-reviewed content and specific disclaimer structures, adding HK$10,000-HK$30,000
- **Custom photography and video**: Hong Kong businesses in premium sectors (finance, luxury, hospitality) often need original visual assets — budget HK$15,000-HK$50,000 for professional shoots in Central, Victoria Peak, or harbour-side locations
- **Speed of delivery**: Rush projects (under 4 weeks) carry a 20-30% premium due to resource prioritization

### Cost Reducers

- **Content readiness**: Having finalized bilingual copy, brand guidelines, and image assets before development starts reduces total cost by 15-25%
- **Phased launches**: Starting with English, then adding Chinese content in phase two, spreads the investment
- **Template foundations**: Using a premium Webflow template as a starting point (with significant customization) can reduce design phase costs by 20-30%

### How much does a bilingual Webflow website cost in Hong Kong?

### How long does a Webflow website take to build in Hong Kong?

A marketing site takes 3-5 weeks, a growth site 6-10 weeks, and an enterprise build 10-16 weeks. Bilingual projects add 1-2 weeks for localization setup and content review. ### Can Webflow handle Traditional Chinese properly?

Yes. Webflow's native localization supports Traditional Chinese with proper character rendering, right-to-left mixed content handling for addresses, and separate URL structures with hreflang tags. This is structurally more reliable than WordPress WPML, which frequently encounters character encoding issues and plugin conflicts when managing Chinese-language content.

### What payment methods can a Webflow e-commerce site support in Hong Kong?

Webflow e-commerce natively supports Stripe, which in Hong Kong processes Visa, Mastercard, American Express, and UnionPay. Additional payment methods like FPS, AlipayHK, and WeChat Pay can be integrated through custom code and third-party payment orchestration layers. The implementation adds to the project cost but provides the payment coverage that Hong Kong and GBA customers expect.
